Introduction

Our oceans, covering over 70% of Earth's surface, are under unprecedented threat. From pollution to overfishing, these challenges endanger marine life and ecosystems vital to global sustainability.

Ocean Pollution

Every year, millions of tons of plastic and chemicals enter the oceans, choking marine life and disrupting ecosystems. The UNEP estimates that 80% of marine pollution comes from land-based sources.

Overfishing

Unsustainable fishing practices have depleted fish stocks, with over 33% of global fisheries exploited beyond sustainable limits, according to the UNDP. This threatens food security and livelihoods.

Climate Change

Rising sea temperatures and ocean acidification, driven by climate change, are bleaching coral reefs and altering marine habitats. The Global Goals initiative highlights the urgent need to address these impacts.

Ocean health is critical to achieving SDG 14. Collective action is essential to mitigate these threats and protect life below water.
